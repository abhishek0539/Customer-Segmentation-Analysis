About Customer Segmentation

Customer segmentation is the practice of dividing a customer base into groups of individuals that are similar in specific ways such as age, gender, interests, and spending habits. This helps businesses target different categories of consumers more effectively.

Definitions

  • RFM: RFM stands for Recency, Frequency, and Monetary analysis.This method is used to segment customers for targeted marketing campaigns such as identifying loyal, at-risk, or new customers.
    • Recency: How recently a customer made a purchase
    • Frequency: How often they make purchases
    • Monetary: How much money they spend
  • Churn Risk: Measures the likelihood of a customer leaving or cancelling a service.
  • Boxplot: Visualizes data distribution using quartiles and highlights outliers.
  • Clusters in KMeans: Groups similar data points by minimizing distance to cluster centroids.
  • Violin Plot: Combines boxplot and KDE to show data distribution and density.
  • Heatmap: Uses color to represent values in a matrix, highlighting relationships or patterns.
  • PCA (Principal Component Analysis): Reduces data dimensions by extracting uncorrelated components with maximum variance.
